The Ultimate Guide to Lauterbrunnen: Valley of 72 Waterfalls

Sarah Jenkins

October 24, 2023 • 12 min read

Nestled in one of the most impressive trough valleys in the Alps, between gigantic rock faces and mountain peaks, Lauterbrunnen is a postcard-perfect destination that feels almost too beautiful to be real. Known as the valley of 72 waterfalls, this Swiss gem offers a unique blend of adrenaline-pumping adventure and serene alpine tranquility.

Getting There and Getting Around

The journey to Lauterbrunnen is as scenic as the destination itself. Most travelers arrive via Interlaken, taking the Berner Oberland Bahn. The train ride offers panoramic views of the Lütschine River and the rising peaks of the Eiger, Mönch, and Jungfrau.

The iconic yellow and blue train winding through the Bernese Oberland region.

Must-See Waterfalls

  • water_drop Staubbach Falls: Plunging almost 300 meters from a hanging valley, this is the most iconic landmark of the village.
  • water_drop Trümmelbach Falls: A series of ten glacial waterfalls inside the mountain, accessible by a tunnel lift.
  • water_drop Mürrenbach Falls: Often overlooked but equally stunning, located near the Stechelberg cable car station.

"The atmosphere in Lauterbrunnen during the golden hour is something I will never forget. As the sun dips behind the cliffs, the waterfalls seem to glow against the darkening rock."

Beyond the Valley Floor

While the valley floor is stunning, the true magic of the region lies in the car-free villages of Mürren and Wengen perched on the cliffs above. A quick cable car ride transports you to a world of traditional wooden chalets and world-class hiking trails.
